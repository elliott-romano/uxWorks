Senior Product Designer: Product Growth & Onboarding

In 2020, DigitalOcean formed a new product working group that operated between the brand and product organizations, focused on improving the experience for new and evaluative developers. As the first dedicated designer, I led the strategy, research, and rollout of a new onboarding experience, ensuring a smoother path from first contact to product activation.

Leading up to 2020, DigitalOcean experienced substantial growth, expanding its offerings beyond cloud servers to a broader suite of products designed for modern cloud development. However, its onboarding experience struggled to keep pace with this shift.
Starting Point
The original user experience reflected DigitalOcean's legacy design: after entering payment details and verifying their email, users faced a lengthy evaluation form before creating a cloud server. This approach became misaligned as the platform's offerings expanded beyond virtual machines to diverse, workflow-specific products.
Initial metrics

Data from the legacy experience revealed that most new DigitalOcean users had less than a year of development experience. Many would try cloud products but often abandoned them shortly after onboarding.

Research

To understand this drop-off, I interviewed over 10 developers who stopped using DigitalOcean within their first month, all with less than a year of development experience, as well as university computer science students. Participants were surveyed on cloud knowledge and asked to navigate the platform from sign-up to evaluate its effectiveness.

Research Findings

Research findings indicated that users are primarily focused on learning programming rather than server or IT management. They often lack experience with deployment and Linux, relying heavily on documentation and trusted guidance . Their main goal is to get their projects running in a shareable, browser-accessible state, recognizing that cloud knowledge is essential for success.

Legacy configuration experience

Deployment Challenges

The server deployment process assumed a high level of technical knowledge, leaving new users overwhelmed and unsure how to proceed. Many gave up when faced with unfamiliar steps and little guidance, leading to major drop-off during setup and access.

MVP Test

The goal was clear: simplify onboarding by reducing decision-making, preconfiguring repetitive setup tasks, and offering clear guidance. Many new users were drawn to DigitalOcean for its community tutorials, relying on the platform's expertise to help them get started.
